Top Destinations

Explore the divine beauty of Uttarakhand's most popular tourist places

Discover Uttarakhand's Treasures

From serene lakes to mighty Himalayan peaks, from spiritual havens to adventure hotspots - Uttarakhand offers diverse experiences for every traveler.

Nainital

The Lake District of India

Nestled amidst the Kumaon Hills, Nainital is a charming hill station built around the pear-shaped Naini Lake. This popular tourist destination is known for its scenic beauty, pleasant climate, and boating experiences.

Naini Lake

The centerpiece of the town, offering boating experiences and stunning views.

Naina Devi Temple

An ancient shrine dedicated to Goddess Naina Devi, located at the northern shore of Naini Lake.

Snow View Point

Offering panoramic views of the snow-capped Himalayan peaks.

Mall Road

The bustling shopping street with local handicrafts, eateries, and stunning lake views.

Best Time to Visit: March to June, September to November
Recommended Stay: 2-3 days

Mussoorie

Queen of the Hills

Perched at an altitude of 2,000 meters, Mussoorie is a popular hill station known for its colonial charm, scenic beauty, and pleasant climate. The town offers stunning views of the Doon Valley and the distant Himalayan ranges.

Mall Road

The heart of Mussoorie with shops, cafes, and magnificent mountain views.

Kempty Falls

A scenic waterfall perfect for picnics and swimming.

Gun Hill

Second highest point in Mussoorie offering panoramic views (accessible by cable car).

Camel's Back Road

A 3 km stretch perfect for morning walks with views of a natural rock formation resembling a camel's back.

Best Time to Visit: April to June, September to November
Recommended Stay: 2-3 days

Rishikesh & Haridwar

Spiritual Havens on the Banks of Ganga

Rishikesh and Haridwar are twin sacred cities located on the banks of the holy Ganges river. While Rishikesh is known as the 'Yoga Capital of the World', Haridwar is one of the seven holiest places for Hindus.

Laxman Jhula & Ram Jhula

Iconic suspension bridges across the Ganges with panoramic views.

Ganga Aarti

The mesmerizing evening ritual at Har Ki Pauri in Haridwar and Triveni Ghat in Rishikesh.

River Rafting

Thrilling white water rafting in the rapids of the Ganges in Rishikesh.

Yoga & Meditation Centers

Numerous ashrams and yoga centers offering spiritual retreats and yoga courses.

Best Time to Visit: September to April
Recommended Stay: 3-4 days

Char Dham

Sacred Pilgrimage Circuit

The Char Dham Yatra is the most sacred Hindu pilgrimage circuit in the Indian Himalayas. The circuit consists of four ancient temples - Yamunotri, Gangotri, Kedarnath, and Badrinath, nestled in the Garhwal region of Uttarakhand.

Kedarnath

One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva, situated at an elevation of 3,583 meters.

Badrinath

Sacred to Lord Vishnu, this temple is nestled between the Nar and Narayan mountain ranges.

Gangotri

The origin of the holy river Ganges and home to the temple of Goddess Ganga.

Yamunotri

The source of the Yamuna River and dedicated to Goddess Yamuna.

Best Time to Visit: May to June, September to October
Recommended Stay: 10-12 days (for complete circuit)

Valley of Flowers & Auli

Nature's Paradise & Adventure Hub

Valley of Flowers, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is an enchanting meadow adorned with endemic alpine flowers and surrounded by snow-capped mountains. Nearby Auli is a premier skiing destination offering breathtaking views of Nanda Devi.

Valley of Flowers

A vibrant national park with over 500 species of wild flowers, offering stunning treks.

Auli Ski Resort

One of India's best skiing destinations with slopes suitable for beginners and professionals.

Hemkund Sahib

A sacred Sikh shrine located at an altitude of 4,632 meters near the Valley of Flowers.

Auli Ropeway

One of Asia's longest ropeways (4 km) connecting Joshimath to Auli with spectacular views.

Best Time to Visit: June to October (Valley), December to March (Auli for skiing)
Recommended Stay: 4-5 days

More Places to Explore

Uttarakhand has countless treasures waiting to be discovered. Here are some other fascinating destinations to consider for your trip.

Jim Corbett National Park

India's oldest national park known for its Bengal tigers and diverse wildlife.

Chopta-Tungnath

Mini Switzerland of Uttarakhand with the highest Shiva temple in the world.

Kausani

Offering spectacular 300 km-wide panoramic view of the Himalayan peaks.

Ranikhet

A serene hill station developed by the British as a summer retreat.
