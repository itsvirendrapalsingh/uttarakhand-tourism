Best Time to Visit

Uttarakhand experiences different climates throughout the year, making certain seasons better for specific destinations and activities.

Summer (March to June)

Ideal for hill stations like Mussoorie, Nainital, and Ranikhet. Temperatures range from 15°C to 30°C with pleasant conditions. Great time for sightseeing, adventure activities, and escaping heat from the plains. Char Dham Yatra season begins in May.

Monsoon (July to September)

Heavy rainfall across the state with possibility of landslides and road blockages. Valley of Flowers blooms during this period (July-August). Not recommended for mountain travel, but lowland areas can be visited with caution.

Autumn (October to November)

One of the best times to visit with clear skies, cool temperatures, and excellent visibility of mountain peaks. Perfect for trekking, photography, and visiting most attractions. Char Dham Yatra concludes in November.

Winter (December to February)

Heavy snowfall in higher regions with temperatures dropping below freezing in many areas. Ideal for snow lovers, skiing in Auli, and experiencing a winter wonderland. Many high-altitude destinations may be inaccessible due to snow.

Tourist Season: For most travelers, the ideal time to visit Uttarakhand is during Spring (March-April), early Summer (May-June), and Autumn (September-November) when the weather is pleasant, and most attractions are accessible.

Getting There & Around

Uttarakhand is well-connected to major cities in India through various modes of transportation. Here's how you can reach and explore this beautiful state:

By Air

  • Dehradun Airport (Jolly Grant): Main airport connecting to Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru, and other major cities
  • Pantnagar Airport: Smaller airport serving Kumaon region
  • Nearest International Airport: Delhi (approx. 250-300 km from various Uttarakhand destinations)

By Train

  • Major Railway Stations: Dehradun, Haridwar, Kathgodam, Rishikesh
  • Well-connected to Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata, and other major cities
  • Toy Train: Heritage narrow-gauge railway from Kathgodam to Ranikhet

By Road

  • State Transport: Regular bus services from Delhi, Chandigarh, Lucknow
  • Private Buses: Luxury and deluxe options available from major cities
  • Well-maintained highways connecting to major destinations

Getting Around

  • State Transport Buses: Connect most towns and tourist places
  • Shared Taxis: Available for intercity travel
  • Private Taxi/Car Rental: Most flexible option, available in all major towns
  • Two-wheelers: Rentals available in tourist hubs for short distances

Travel Tip

Book transportation well in advance, especially during peak season (May-June and October-November). Roads to higher altitudes may be closed during heavy snowfall in winter and landslides during monsoon.

Where to Stay

Uttarakhand offers diverse accommodation options to suit every budget and preference. From luxury resorts to homestays, here are your options:

Hotels & Resorts

All major tourist destinations offer a range of hotels from budget to luxury. Hill stations like Mussoorie and Nainital have heritage properties and luxury resorts with spectacular views.

Homestays & Guesthouses

Experience local hospitality and authentic Pahadi culture with homestays in villages and smaller towns. These offer home-cooked meals and insights into local lifestyle.

Camps & Cottages

Riverside camps in Rishikesh, jungle lodges near Jim Corbett, and adventure camps in various locations offer unique experiences close to nature.

Ashrams & Retreats

Rishikesh and Haridwar have numerous ashrams and spiritual retreats offering simple accommodation along with yoga and meditation facilities.

Government Accommodations

GMVN (Garhwal Mandal Vikas Nigam) and KMVN (Kumaon Mandal Vikas Nigam) offer reliable, budget-friendly tourist rest houses across the state, including remote locations.

Accommodation Tip

Book accommodations 2-3 months in advance for peak seasons. For Char Dham Yatra, advance booking is essential. Many high-altitude accommodations close during winter months.

Local Cuisine

Uttarakhand cuisine is hearty, nutritious, and deeply influenced by the mountain lifestyle. Simple cooking techniques and locally grown ingredients create distinctive flavors that you must try:

Must-Try Traditional Dishes

  • Aloo ke Gutke: Spicy potato dish with regional herbs and spices
  • Kafuli: Nutritious spinach preparation cooked with rice flour
  • Bhatt ki Churkani: Black soybean preparation with rich gravy
  • Mandua ki Roti: Nutritious flatbread made from finger millet
  • Rus: Traditional dal preparation with unique flavors
  • Garhwali Kadhi: Yogurt-based curry with gram flour dumplings
  • Phaanu: A mix of different lentils with subtle spices

Sweets & Snacks

  • Bal Mithai: Chocolate-like fudge coated with white sugar balls
  • Singodi: Sweet made from khoya wrapped in malu leaf
  • Jhangora ki Kheer: Pudding made from barnyard millet
  • Arsa: Sweet deep-fried rice flour cookies
  • Gulgula: Sweet fried dumplings made during festivals

Where to Eat

For authentic local cuisine, try small local eateries in towns and villages rather than touristy restaurants. Many homestays offer home-cooked Pahadi meals. Most tourist destinations also have restaurants serving North Indian, Chinese, and Continental cuisines.

Food Tip

The cuisine varies between Garhwal and Kumaon regions, offering unique specialties. Look for seasonal dishes like Lingri (fern) in spring and various preparations using local millets and legumes.

Travel Essentials

Being well-prepared will enhance your Uttarakhand experience. Here's what you need to know before you go:

What to Pack

  • Comfortable walking shoes/trekking boots
  • Layered clothing (temperatures vary widely)
  • Rain gear (umbrella/raincoat)
  • Sunscreen, sunglasses, and hat
  • Basic first-aid kit with altitude sickness medication
  • Warm clothes (even in summer for high altitudes)
  • Power bank (electricity can be unreliable in remote areas)

Money Matters

  • ATMs available in major towns but scarce in remote areas
  • Carry sufficient cash, especially for small towns and villages
  • Credit cards accepted in hotels and bigger establishments
  • Mobile payment options like PayTM widely accepted
  • Budget extra for unexpected expenses like weather delays

Connectivity

  • Mobile network coverage varies by location
  • BSNL has best coverage in remote areas
  • Download offline maps before heading to remote places
  • Inform family about your itinerary if visiting remote areas
  • WiFi available in most hotels but may be slow

Health & Safety

  • Consult doctor before visiting high altitudes
  • Drink bottled/filtered water only
  • Medical facilities limited in remote areas
  • Travel insurance highly recommended
  • Acclimatize properly before heading to high altitudes
  • Avoid traveling during heavy rainfall in monsoon

Suggested Itineraries

Here are some recommended itineraries to help you plan your Uttarakhand journey based on your interests and available time:

5-6 Days

Classic Kumaon Circuit

  • Day 1: Arrive in Kathgodam/Haldwani, proceed to Nainital
  • Day 2: Explore Nainital - boating, shopping, viewpoints
  • Day 3: Day trip to Bhimtal, Sattal, and Naukuchiatal lakes
  • Day 4: Travel to Kausani, enjoy sunset views of Himalayas
  • Day 5: Visit Baijnath Temple, tea gardens
  • Day 6: Return to Kathgodam/Haldwani

Perfect for: First-time visitors, families, leisure travelers

5-6 Days

Spiritual Journey

  • Day 1: Arrive in Haridwar, witness Ganga Aarti
  • Day 2: Explore Haridwar temples, proceed to Rishikesh
  • Day 3: Rishikesh temples, ashrams, and Ganga Aarti
  • Day 4: Yoga/meditation session, visit Beatles Ashram
  • Day 5: Day trip to Devprayag or Neelkanth Mahadev
  • Day 6: Return journey

Perfect for: Spiritual seekers, yoga enthusiasts, soul searchers

8-10 Days

Adventure Trail

  • Day 1-2: Rishikesh - rafting, cliff jumping, camping
  • Day 3-4: Chopta and Tungnath trek
  • Day 5-6: Valley of Flowers trek (seasonal)
  • Day 7-8: Auli - skiing (winter) or cable car experience
  • Day 9-10: Return journey with stops at Devprayag and Haridwar

Perfect for: Adventure enthusiasts, nature lovers, active travelers

12-14 Days

Char Dham Yatra

  • Day 1-2: Haridwar/Rishikesh acclimatization
  • Day 3-4: Yamunotri visit
  • Day 5-6: Gangotri visit
  • Day 7-9: Kedarnath visit (includes trekking)
  • Day 10-11: Badrinath visit
  • Day 12-14: Return journey with rest stops

Perfect for: Pilgrims, spiritual seekers, those seeking a comprehensive sacred journey
